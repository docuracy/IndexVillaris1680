Without specialist local knowledge, it has proved impossible to identify the locations of about 5% of the 24,000 place-names recorded by John Adams in his Index Villaris. Difficulties are presented by combinations of unconventional spellings, incorrectly assigned counties or hundreds (county subdivisions), and sometimes by transcription or typographical errors in either or both of the geographical coordinates.
